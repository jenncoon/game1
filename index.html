What You Are Looking At

Games designed to make students think, not just click.

These games require students to reason, evaluate, and make judgments, not recall definitions. Each game is designed around the specific concepts where students lose the thread, and requires genuine thinking to complete. The mechanics change by chapter. The purpose does not.

How These Were Built

Claude AI as a coding assistant.

The pedagogical decisions, concept targets, student struggle points, and assessment philosophy came entirely from the instructor. Claude was used as an execution partner to build and refine the HTML and JavaScript based on those decisions.

Step 01
🧠

Instructor designs the experience

Which student struggle to target, what cognitive demand to aim for, how scoring should work, what the reflection should ask. All instructor decisions.

Step 02
🤖

Claude builds the game

Writing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to the instructor's specification. Proposing question answer choices for review. Building scoring logic and download mechanics.

Step 03
🔁

Test, review, revise

Each game goes through multiple rounds of testing and revision. The iteration is not uncertainty about what to build. It is the normal work of getting instructional design right.
